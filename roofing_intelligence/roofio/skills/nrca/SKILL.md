# NRCA (National Roofing Contractors Association) Skill

## ROOFIO - Division 07 Expert System

### Overview
NRCA is the primary authority for roofing best practices in the United States. The NRCA Roofing Manual is the industry bible. This skill covers NRCA guidelines, standards, and best practices.

## NRCA Flashing Guidelines

### Base Flashing Requirements

**Minimum Heights:**
```
Standard condition:     8" above roof surface
At equipment curbs:     8" minimum (12" recommended)
Within 10' of drain:    12" minimum for curbs
At area dividers:       8" minimum each side
```

**Termination Methods:**
1. Counter flashing (2-piece system)
2. Reglet with sealant
3. Surface-mounted termination bar
4. Through-wall flashing

### Counter Flashing

**Masonry Walls:**
- Cut reglet 1" deep minimum
- Stainless steel or copper recommended
- Hemmed edge into reglet
- Sealant at top and overlaps
- 4" minimum overlap at joints

**Metal Stud/EIFS:**
- Surface-mounted typical
- Coordinate with air barrier
- Continuous sealant bed
- Mechanical fastening required

**Concrete:**
- Reglet cast-in or cut
- Adhesive and/or fasteners
- Backer rod and sealant termination

### Edge Metal

**Gravel Stop:**
- 4" minimum face height
- Continuous cleat
- Water dam at membrane
- 4' maximum joint spacing

**Drip Edge/Fascia:**
- Hem at outer edge
- Cleat or concealed fastening
- Expansion joints at 10' OC (aluminum)
- Thermal breaks for dissimilar metals

**Coping:**
- Cover entire parapet top
- Minimum 2:12 slope to interior
- Splice plates at joints
- 10' max length (steel), 8' (aluminum)

## NRCA Membrane Specifications

### TPO (Thermoplastic Polyolefin)

**Membrane Thickness:**
| Application | Minimum | Recommended |
|-------------|---------|-------------|
| Adhered field | 45 mil | 60 mil |
| Mechanically attached | 45 mil | 60-80 mil |
| Ballasted | 45 mil | 60 mil |
| Walkway areas | 60 mil | 80 mil |

**Seam Requirements:**
- Minimum 2.5" wide weld
- Probe test 100% of seams
- T-patches at intersections
- No seams within 6" of penetrations

**Temperature Limits:**
- Installation: 40°F minimum (membrane)
- Hot-air welding: 40°F minimum ambient
- Adhesive: Per manufacturer (typically 40-50°F)

### EPDM (Ethylene Propylene Diene Monomer)

**Membrane Thickness:**
| Application | Minimum | Recommended |
|-------------|---------|-------------|
| Adhered | 45 mil | 60 mil |
| Mechanically attached | 45 mil | 60 mil |
| Ballasted | 45 mil | 60 mil |
| High-traffic | 60 mil | 90 mil |

**Seam Methods:**
- Tape seams (most common)
- Liquid adhesive seams
- Minimum 3" seam width
- Prime before tape/adhesive

**Ballast Requirements:**
- Minimum 10 psf (river-washed)
- Minimum 12 psf (crushed stone)
- Pavers: 18-22 psf typical
- Edge restraint required

### PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride)

**Membrane Thickness:**
| Application | Minimum | Recommended |
|-------------|---------|-------------|
| Adhered | 50 mil | 60-80 mil |
| Mechanically attached | 50 mil | 60-80 mil |
| High chemical exposure | 60 mil | 80 mil |

**Chemical Resistance:**
- Excellent for grease, oils
- Recommended for restaurants
- Protect from asphalt contact
- Separation required from polystyrene

### Modified Bitumen

**Application Methods:**
| Method | Temperature | Equipment |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Torch-applied | Propane flame | Torch, fire watch |
| Cold-applied | 40°F+ | Rollers, squeegees |
| Self-adhered | 50°F+ | Rollers |
| Hot-mopped | 350-425°F | Kettle, mop |

**Ply Configuration:**
```
2-Ply System (standard):
- Base sheet (Type VI or self-adhered)
- Cap sheet (mineral or smooth)

3-Ply System (enhanced):
- Base sheet
- Interply sheet
- Cap sheet
```

### Built-Up Roofing (BUR)

**Standard Configurations:**
```
3-Ply: 3 plies felt, flood coat, gravel
4-Ply: 4 plies felt, flood coat, gravel
Smooth: Multiple plies, coating (no gravel)
```

**Asphalt Types:**
| Type | Softening Point | Slope |
|------|-----------------|-------|
| I | 135-150°F | 0-1/2" per foot |
| II | 158-176°F | 1/4-1" per foot |
| III | 185-205°F | 1/2-3" per foot |
| IV | 210-225°F | Over 1" per foot |

## NRCA Insulation Guidelines

### Thermal Performance

**R-Value Requirements by Climate Zone:**
| Zone | Above Deck | Metal Building |
|------|------------|----------------|
| 1 | R-20 ci | R-19 + R-11 |
| 2 | R-20 ci | R-19 + R-11 |
| 3 | R-20 ci | R-19 + R-11 |
| 4 | R-25 ci | R-19 + R-11 |
| 5 | R-30 ci | R-25 + R-11 |
| 6 | R-30 ci | R-25 + R-11 |
| 7-8 | R-35 ci | R-30 + R-13 |

*ci = continuous insulation*

### Insulation Types

**Polyisocyanurate (Polyiso):**
- R-5.6 to R-6.0 per inch (aged)
- Most common for commercial
- Temperature-dependent performance
- LTTR (Long-Term Thermal Resistance)

**Expanded Polystyrene (EPS):**
- R-3.6 to R-4.2 per inch
- Moisture tolerant
- Used below membrane, under ballast
- Protected membrane assemblies

**Extruded Polystyrene (XPS):**
- R-5.0 per inch
- Excellent moisture resistance
- Plaza deck assemblies
- Not compatible with PVC membrane

**Mineral Wool/Stone Wool:**
- R-4.2 per inch
- Non-combustible (FM Class 1)
- High compressive strength
- Excellent fire resistance

### Installation Requirements

**General:**
- Joints offset minimum 6"
- Multiple layers: stagger 6" minimum
- No more than 1/4" gaps
- Fill voids with compatible material
- Mechanically attached or adhered per wind design

**Tapered Insulation:**
- Minimum 1/8" per foot slope
- Cricket/saddle at equipment
- Valleys drain to sumps
- Transition to flat at walls

## NRCA Drain Guidelines

### Primary Drains

**Sizing:**
```
Minimum: 4" diameter
Recommended: 6" for most commercial
Large roofs: Multiple drains, sized per rainfall
```

**Spacing:**
- Maximum drainage distance: 50-75 feet
- One drain per 10,000 SF (general rule)
- Confirm with local rainfall intensity

**Installation:**
- Set in sump (4" minimum depth)
- Clamping ring over membrane
- Strainer/dome required
- Flashing integrated with membrane

### Overflow/Secondary Drainage

**Code Requirement:**
- Required by IBC for enclosed buildings
- Capacity equal to primary system
- Independent of primary drains
- 2" above primary inlet

**Types:**
- Overflow drains (internal leaders)
- Scuppers (through parapet)
- Combination systems

**Scupper Sizing:**
| Area | Minimum Size |
|------|--------------|
| <2,500 SF | 4" × 4" |
| 2,500-5,000 SF | 4" × 7" |
| 5,000-10,000 SF | 6" × 6" |
| >10,000 SF | Multiple or larger |

## NRCA Quality Standards

### Acceptable Tolerances

**Ponding Water:**
- NRCA Definition: Water remaining 48 hours after precipitation
- Design should minimize ponding
- Some ponding acceptable per manufacturer

**Surface Irregularities:**
- Membrane wrinkles: Minimize, avoid bridging
- Fishmouths: Not acceptable, must repair
- Blisters: Evaluate size, repair if necessary

**Seam Quality:**
- 100% bonded (no voids)
- Minimum width met
- No burn-through (heat weld)
- No edge lifting

### Inspection Requirements

**Pre-Installation:**
- [ ] Deck condition acceptable
- [ ] Slope adequate
- [ ] Penetrations in place
- [ ] Substrate dry

**During Installation:**
- [ ] Material storage proper
- [ ] Seam quality checked
- [ ] Flashing heights verified
- [ ] Fastener patterns correct

**Final Inspection:**
- [ ] All seams probed
- [ ] Flashings complete
- [ ] Drains functional
- [ ] Debris removed
- [ ] Documentation complete

## NRCA Warranty Guidance

### Warranty Types

**Manufacturer's Material Warranty:**
- Covers material defects only
- 10-30 years typical
- Limited remedy (repair/replace material)

**Manufacturer's System Warranty:**
- Covers materials and labor
- Requires certified installer
- 10-25 years typical
- NDL (No Dollar Limit) available

**Contractor Warranty:**
- Workmanship warranty
- 1-5 years typical
- Labor for repairs

### Warranty Requirements

**Standard NDL Requirements:**
- Pre-approved contractor
- Shop drawing submittal
- Progress inspections
- Final inspection
- Maintenance plan

**Common Exclusions:**
- Consequential damages
- Ponding water (some)
- Punctures, abuse
- Unauthorized repairs
- Acts of God
