# Roofing Systems Comprehensive Guide

## ROOFIO - Division 07 Expert System

### Overview
Complete reference for all commercial and residential roofing systems, including materials, installation methods, advantages, limitations, and applications.

---

## LOW-SLOPE SYSTEMS (<3:12)

### TPO (Thermoplastic Polyolefin)

**Material:**
- Polymer blend (polypropylene + ethylene-propylene)
- Reinforced with polyester or fiberglass scrim
- Available: 45, 60, 80 mil thickness

**Installation Methods:**
| Method | Description | Wind Rating |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Mechanically attached | Fasteners in seams | FM 1-60 to 1-240 |
| Fully adhered | Bonding adhesive | FM 1-90 to 1-300 |
| Ballasted | Stone or pavers | Limited wind areas |

**Seaming:** Hot-air welded (900-1100°F)

**Advantages:**
- Energy efficient (white reflective)
- Weldable seams (strong)
- Chemical resistant (moderate)
- UV stable
- Lower cost than PVC

**Limitations:**
- Younger technology (30+ year history)
- Less chemical resistance than PVC
- Requires experienced welders

**Typical Life:** 20-30 years

---

### PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride)

**Material:**
- PVC polymer with plasticizers
- Reinforced with polyester scrim
- Available: 50, 60, 80 mil thickness

**Installation Methods:**
| Method | Description | Wind Rating |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Mechanically attached | Fasteners in seams | FM 1-60 to 1-240 |
| Fully adhered | Bonding adhesive | FM 1-90 to 1-300 |
| Ballasted | Stone or pavers | Limited wind areas |

**Seaming:** Hot-air welded (automatic or hand)

**Advantages:**
- Excellent chemical resistance
- Fire resistant (self-extinguishing)
- Long track record (50+ years)
- Weldable seams
- Available in colors

**Limitations:**
- Higher cost than TPO
- NOT compatible with asphalt/bitumen
- NOT compatible with polystyrene
- Plasticizer migration (older formulations)

**Typical Life:** 25-35 years

---

### EPDM (Ethylene Propylene Diene Monomer)

**Material:**
- Synthetic rubber compound
- Non-reinforced or reinforced
- Available: 45, 60, 90 mil thickness

**Installation Methods:**
| Method | Description | Wind Rating |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Mechanically attached | Bars or plates | FM 1-60 to 1-150 |
| Fully adhered | Contact adhesive | FM 1-90 to 1-180 |
| Ballasted | Stone (10-12 psf) | Limited wind areas |

**Seaming:** Tape or liquid adhesive

**Advantages:**
- Excellent flexibility
- UV/ozone resistant
- Long track record (50+ years)
- Lower cost
- Wide sheets available (50')

**Limitations:**
- Black absorbs heat (white available)
- Seams not as strong as welds
- Requires primers and adhesives
- Puncture susceptible

**Typical Life:** 20-30 years

---

### Modified Bitumen

**Material:**
- Asphalt modified with polymers
- SBS (Styrene-Butadiene-Styrene) - flexible
- APP (Atactic Polypropylene) - heat resistant
- Reinforced with fiberglass or polyester

**Installation Methods:**
| Method | Temperature | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| Torch-applied | Propane flame | Most common |
| Cold-applied | Adhesive | No flame |
| Self-adhered | Peel and stick | >40°F |
| Hot-mopped | Hot asphalt | Traditional |

**System Configurations:**
```
2-Ply Standard:
- Base sheet (Type VI or SA)
- Cap sheet (mineral or smooth)

3-Ply Enhanced:
- Base sheet
- Interply sheet
- Cap sheet
```

**Advantages:**
- Proven technology
- Multiple application options
- Good puncture resistance
- Self-healing (some damage)
- Compatible with BUR

**Limitations:**
- Hot work (fire risk)
- Heavy
- Skilled labor required
- Temperature sensitive

**Typical Life:** 20-30 years

---

### Built-Up Roofing (BUR)

**Material:**
- Multiple plies of felt
- Asphalt between layers
- Surfacing (gravel, cap sheet, coating)

**System Configurations:**
```
3-Ply: 3 felt layers + flood coat + gravel
4-Ply: 4 felt layers + flood coat + gravel
Smooth: Multiple plies + cap sheet or coating
```

**Asphalt Types:**
| Type | Softening Point | Slope |
|------|-----------------|-------|
| I | 135-150°F | 0-1/2:12 |
| II | 158-176°F | 1/4-1:12 |
| III | 185-205°F | 1/2-3:12 |
| IV | 210-225°F | >1:12 |

**Advantages:**
- Longest track record (100+ years)
- Redundant layers
- Easy to maintain
- Good fire resistance
- Familiar to contractors

**Limitations:**
- Labor intensive
- Hot kettles (safety)
- Heavy
- Fumes/odors
- Limited slope capability

**Typical Life:** 25-40 years

---

### Spray Polyurethane Foam (SPF)

**Material:**
- Closed-cell polyurethane foam
- Sprayed in place
- Requires elastomeric coating

**Application:**
1. Surface preparation
2. Primer (if required)
3. Spray foam (1-3" typical)
4. Protective coating (silicone, acrylic)
5. Granules in coating (optional)

**Advantages:**
- Seamless membrane
- Excellent insulation (R-6.5/inch)
- Lightweight
- Conforms to irregular shapes
- Self-flashing

**Limitations:**
- Weather-dependent application
- Skilled applicators required
- Coating maintenance required
- Overspray concerns
- UV protection required

**Typical Life:** 25-30 years (with coating maintenance)

---

### Liquid-Applied Membranes

**Types:**
| Type | Chemistry | Application |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| Silicone | Single-component | Spray/roll |
| Acrylic | Water-based | Roll/spray |
| Urethane | Two-component | Spray |
| PMMA | Methyl methacrylate | Roll |

**Advantages:**
- Seamless
- Easy application
- Conforms to complex shapes
- Good for re-cover
- Various thicknesses

**Limitations:**
- Weather dependent
- Multiple coats required
- Thickness control critical
- Surface prep crucial

**Typical Life:** 15-25 years (depends on type)

## STEEP-SLOPE SYSTEMS (>3:12)

### Asphalt Shingles

**Types:**
| Type | Composition | Life |
|------|-------------|------|
| 3-Tab | Fiberglass mat, single layer | 15-20 yrs |
| Architectural | Laminated, dimensional | 25-30 yrs |
| Premium | Heavy laminated | 30-50 yrs |

**Installation:**
- Minimum slope: 4:12 (2:12 with underlayment)
- Nail pattern: 4-6 nails per shingle
- Offset: 6" minimum stagger

**Underlayment:**
- Felt (15# or 30#)
- Synthetic
- Self-adhered (ice dam areas)

---

### Metal Roofing

**Panel Types:**
| Type | Profile | Attachment |
|------|---------|------------|
| Standing seam | Raised seams | Concealed clips |
| Exposed fastener | Ribbed | Through-fastened |
| Metal shingles | Shingle look | Concealed nailing |

**Materials:**
| Material | Gauge | Life |
|----------|-------|------|
| Galvanized steel | 24-26 ga | 30-50 yrs |
| Galvalume | 24-26 ga | 40-60 yrs |
| Aluminum | 0.032-0.040" | 40-60 yrs |
| Copper | 16-20 oz | 75-100+ yrs |
| Zinc | Various | 75-100+ yrs |

**Advantages:**
- Long life
- Fire resistant
- Lightweight
- Energy efficient (reflective coatings)
- Recyclable

**Limitations:**
- Higher initial cost
- Expansion/contraction
- Oil canning potential
- Fastener maintenance (exposed)
- Noise (some applications)

---

### Tile Roofing

**Types:**
| Type | Material | Weight |
|------|----------|--------|
| Clay | Fired clay | 9-12 psf |
| Concrete | Portland cement | 9-12 psf |
| Synthetic | Composite | 2-5 psf |

**Installation:**
- Minimum slope: 3:12 to 4:12
- Underlayment critical
- Battens or direct deck

**Advantages:**
- 50-100+ year life
- Fire resistant (Class A)
- Aesthetic appeal
- Wind resistant when installed properly

**Limitations:**
- Heavy (structural requirements)
- Fragile (walking damage)
- Cost
- Skilled installation required

---

### Slate Roofing

**Grades:**
| Grade | Life Expectancy |
|-------|-----------------|
| S1 (hard) | 75-100+ years |
| S2 (medium) | 40-75 years |
| S3 (soft) | 20-40 years |

**Installation:**
- Minimum slope: 4:12
- Headlap: 3-4"
- Copper nails recommended

**Advantages:**
- 100+ year life possible
- Natural beauty
- Fire resistant
- Extremely durable

**Limitations:**
- Expensive
- Very heavy
- Fragile
- Specialized installation
- Structural requirements

---

### Wood Shingles/Shakes

**Types:**
| Type | Cut | Thickness |
|------|-----|-----------|
| Shingle | Sawn | Uniform |
| Shake | Split | Varies |

**Wood Species:**
- Western red cedar (most common)
- Alaskan yellow cedar
- Eastern white cedar
- Redwood

**Installation:**
- Minimum slope: 3:12 (shingles), 4:12 (shakes)
- Spaced sheathing acceptable
- Interlayment for shakes

**Fire Treatment:**
- Untreated: Class C
- Pressure treated: Class B or A
- Fire retardant: Required in many areas

## SPECIALTY SYSTEMS

### Vegetated (Green) Roofs

**Types:**
| Type | Depth | Weight | Plants |
|------|-------|--------|--------|
| Extensive | 2-6" | 15-50 psf | Sedum, grass |
| Intensive | 6-24"+ | 50-150+ psf | Varied |

**Components:**
1. Waterproof membrane (root resistant)
2. Root barrier
3. Protection/drainage layer
4. Filter fabric
5. Growing medium
6. Vegetation

**Advantages:**
- Stormwater management
- Urban heat reduction
- Extended membrane life
- Aesthetic
- LEED points

---

### Cool Roofs

**Requirements (IECC):**
- Solar reflectance: ≥0.70 initial, ≥0.55 3-year
- Thermal emittance: ≥0.75
- OR SRI ≥82 initial, ≥64 3-year

**Options:**
- White single-ply (TPO, PVC, EPDM)
- Cool coatings
- Reflective metal
- Cool-colored granules

---

### Protected Membrane (IRMA/PMR)

**Configuration:**
```
Top to bottom:
- Ballast (pavers or stone)
- Filter fabric
- Insulation (XPS)
- Waterproof membrane
- Roof deck
```

**Advantages:**
- Membrane protected
- Easy repairs
- Plaza deck applications

**Limitations:**
- Insulation exposure
- Thermal bridging potential
- Weight

## System Selection Guide

### By Application

| Building Type | Recommended Systems |
|---------------|---------------------|
| Warehouse | TPO, EPDM, Metal |
| Office | TPO, PVC, Mod Bit |
| Restaurant | PVC (chemical resistance) |
| Hospital | TPO, PVC, Mod Bit |
| Retail | TPO, EPDM, Metal |
| Industrial | PVC, Metal |
| Residential | Shingles, Metal, Tile |

### By Priority

| Priority | Best Options |
|----------|--------------|
| Lowest cost | EPDM, 3-tab shingles |
| Longest life | Metal, slate, tile |
| Energy efficiency | TPO, PVC, cool metal |
| Fire resistance | Metal, tile, PVC |
| Chemical resistance | PVC |
| Green/sustainable | Vegetated, metal |
